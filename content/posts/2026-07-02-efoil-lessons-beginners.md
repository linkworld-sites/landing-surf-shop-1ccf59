Here's the thing about eFoil lessons: most people expect to spend the whole session falling off the board.

Most people are wrong.

Our beginner lessons are structured around one goal — getting you actually flying, not just floating. By the end of a standard intro session, the majority of our first-timers have experienced at least a few seconds of genuine foil flight. Some stick it for minutes at a time. A handful immediately book their next session before they've dried off.

That's what learning the right way looks like.

## What Happens in a Beginner eFoil Lesson

### Step 1: The Briefing (10 minutes, on land)

No boring safety lectures. We cover the three things that matter: how the remote works, what the board does when you get the speed right, and how to fall safely. That's it. We've trimmed everything else.

You'll hold the remote before you ever touch the water so there are no surprises when it counts.

### Step 2: Board Feel — Prone and Kneeling (10–15 minutes)

Your first minutes on the board are spent lying down or on your knees. This sounds unglamorous. It is, briefly. It's also the fastest way to build a feel for the foil's response without overloading your brain with balance work.

You'll learn the power band — where in the throttle range the board starts to lift — and you'll feel the foil engage under your weight. By the time you stand up, this feels familiar.

### Step 3: First Stands and Finding Your Footing (15–20 minutes)

Standing up on an eFoil is different from standing up on a surfboard. There's no wave to catch — the power is already there. Your job is to get your feet over the foil, stay centered, and let the board rise.

Your instructor is in the water with you for this entire phase — positioned where they can give you real-time feedback and grab the board if something goes wrong. You're never out there guessing alone.

### Step 4: Foil Time (as long as you've got)

Once you're standing and finding lift, the session is yours. Your instructor moves to a follow position and coaches from a slight distance. You work on height, stability, and — once you're comfortable — direction changes.

This is where it clicks. This is the part you'll replay in your head later.

## Our Approach to Safety

Safety at a water sports venue usually means a wall of disclaimers and an aggressive wavier. That's not us.

Our safety approach is practical:

- **We start shallow.** Your first board time happens in calm, shallow water where you can stand up at any moment.
- **Impact gear, always.** Helmet and impact vest on every rider, every session — no exceptions.
- **Instructor in the water.** Not on shore with binoculars. In the water, within arm's reach during the learning phase.
- **Speed is controlled.** Beginner boards are configured with a lower power ceiling. You physically cannot accidentally go too fast.
- **We watch the conditions.** If the wind or swell picks up to a level that makes learning harder, we'll tell you before you get in the water — not after.

We've had zero serious incidents across hundreds of beginner sessions. That's not luck. It's how we run them.

## Is eFoiling Hard to Learn?

Honestly? It's harder than it looks on Instagram and easier than you think it's going to be.

The foil itself is forgiving. It wants to lift — that's what it's designed to do. Your job is mostly about not fighting it. The riders who learn fastest are the ones who stay relaxed, keep their weight centered, and stop overthinking.

Former skaters and snowboarders tend to pick it up quickly — the edge-feel translates. Surfers sometimes take longer because they keep waiting for a wave that isn't coming. True beginners with no board sports background are often our best students.

**You don't need to be fit.** You don't need to be young. You don't need to be a water person. You need to be willing to fall in a few times and get back on.

## How Fast Will I Progress?

Everyone's different, but here's what a realistic timeline looks like:

| Session | What Most Riders Achieve |
|---------|--------------------------|
| **Session 1** | Standing on the board, first foil lift, 3–10 seconds of flight |
| **Session 2** | Consistent foil height, beginning directional control |
| **Session 3–5** | Carving turns, speed exploration, riding independently |
| **Session 6+** | Personal style, advanced technique, starting to look good |

Some people compress this significantly. Some take longer. Neither is wrong.

## What to Wear and Bring

- **Swimwear** that you don't mind getting completely soaked
- **A towel** — you will use it
- **Sunscreen** (or we'll all regret it)
- **Leave the watches, jewelry, and GoPro mounts** for after you can actually foil

We provide wetsuits if the water calls for it. Everything else — board, remote, helmet, vest — is included.
